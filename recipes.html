Chicken Biryani (Lahori Style)

1 hour 30 mins
Serves 4-6
4.9 (128 reviews)
Medium

This authentic Lahori Chicken Biryani recipe brings the flavors of Pakistan's most famous dish to your kitchen. Layers of fragrant basmati rice and spiced chicken cooked to perfection with traditional methods.

Ingredients

For Chicken Marination:

  • Chicken (with bones) 1 kg
  • Yogurt 1 cup
  • Ginger-garlic paste 2 tbsp
  • Lemon juice 2 tbsp
  • Red chili powder 1 tsp
  • Turmeric powder 1/2 tsp
  • Garam masala 1 tsp
  • Biryani masala 2 tbsp
  • Salt to taste

For Rice:

  • Basmati rice 500g
  • Bay leaves 2
  • Black cardamom 2
  • Cinnamon stick 1 inch
  • Cloves 4-5
  • Salt 1 tbsp

For Layering:

  • Onions (sliced) 3 large
  • Tomatoes (chopped) 2 medium
  • Green chilies 4-5
  • Fresh coriander 1/2 cup
  • Fresh mint 1/2 cup
  • Saffron 1/4 tsp
  • Warm milk 1/4 cup
  • Ghee 1/2 cup

Instructions

  1. Marinate the chicken: In a large bowl, mix chicken with all marination ingredients. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours (overnight for best results).
  2. Prepare the rice: Wash and soak basmati rice for 30 minutes. In a large pot, bring water to boil with whole spices and salt. Add rice and cook until 70% done (about 5-6 minutes). Drain and set aside.
  3. Fry onions: Heat ghee in a pan and fry sliced onions until golden brown. Remove half for garnishing.
  4. Cook chicken: In the same pan with remaining onions, add marinated chicken. Cook on high heat for 5 minutes, then simmer for 15-20 minutes until chicken is tender and gravy thickens.
  5. Prepare saffron milk: Soak saffron in warm milk for 15 minutes.
  6. Layering: In a heavy-bottomed pot, layer half of the rice, then all the chicken with gravy, then remaining rice. Sprinkle fried onions, mint, coriander, and saffron milk on top.
  7. Dum cooking: Cover with tight lid (seal with dough if needed) and cook on very low heat for 20-25 minutes.
  8. Resting: Turn off heat and let it rest for 10 minutes before serving.
  9. Serving: Mix gently and serve with raita, salad, and lemon wedges.

Chef's Notes:

  • For authentic flavor, use bone-in chicken pieces (thighs and legs work best).
  • The quality of basmati rice is crucial - use aged long-grain basmati.
  • Don't skip the "dum" (steam) process - this is what makes biryani special.
  • If you don't have saffron, you can use yellow food color mixed with milk.
  • Leftover biryani tastes even better the next day!
